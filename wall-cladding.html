WALL CLADDING

Porcelain Cladding for Modern Architecture

Large-format porcelain panels from Laminam combine architectural presence with exceptional durability. Used in high-traffic environments like retail flagships, offices, and airports, they deliver seamless elegance with minimal joints and maximum impact.

Whether for interior walls or exterior façades, our porcelain cladding offers a refined, commercial-grade finish that meets fire codes, resists wear, and maintains colour stability for decades.

Why Porcelain Performs

Laminam’s large-format slabs are engineered to outperform — with near-zero water absorption, high impact resistance, and full fire classification for public spaces. At just 3–12 mm thick, they deliver lightweight durability that exceeds many traditional cladding materials.

Backed by UV stability, chemical resistance, and a hygienic non-porous surface (with optional TiO₂ coating), these panels are ideal for commercial interiors and façades that demand beauty, cleanliness, and long-term resilience.

Performance in Practice

From retail flagships to hospital interiors, Laminam’s large-format porcelain slabs prove their strength in the most demanding commercial spaces. Designed to withstand high traffic, impact, and intense cleaning cycles, they outperform traditional cladding materials across every key metric.

Whether overlaid onto existing finishes or installed from slab, each panel delivers chemical resistance, thermal stability, fire safety, and a hygienic, low-maintenance surface that holds up for decades.

Commercial Use Cases

Laminam porcelain cladding is trusted across sectors that demand durability, cleanliness, and visual impact — from global retail brands to hospital intensive care units.

In retail, it guides wayfinding while resisting footfall and trolley traffic. In offices and airports, it overlays outdated surfaces without structural disruption. And in healthcare or hospitality, its non-porous surface ensures both hygiene and style.

Lifecycle & ROI

Laminam cladding isn’t just beautiful — it’s a smart investment. While the material cost is higher than basic surfaces, its strength, colourfastness and scratch resistance dramatically reduce the need for maintenance or replacement over time.

With no sealing required and simple detergent cleaning, operating costs stay low. Overlays avoid disruptive demolition, allowing commercial spaces to stay open — saving both time and revenue.
